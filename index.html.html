Famous Location in Pampanga

Philipines Pampanga Travelers

Pampanga, located in the Central Luzon region of the Philippines, offers a rich tapestry of cultural, historical, and culinary attractions for tourists. Here are some of the top tourist destinations in Pampanga.

Where in Pampanga

1.Mount Pinatubo: While technically located in the boundaries of Pampanga, Tarlac, and Zambales, Mount Pinatubo is a popular destination for adventurous travelers. Visitors can embark on a trek to its crater lake, which formed after its cataclysmic eruption in 1991.

2.San Guillermo Parish Church: Also known as the Bacolor Church, this Baroque-style church is famous for its partially-buried structure due to the eruption of Mount Pinatubo. Despite being partially buried in lahar, the church remains operational and serves as a testament to the resilience of the local community.

3.Puning Hot Springs: Located in Sitio Target, Sapang Bato, Angeles City, Puning Hot Springs offers visitors a unique experience of relaxing in natural hot springs amidst the breathtaking landscape of lahar canyons and Mount Pinatubo.

4.Clark Freeport Zone: Formerly the site of a US military base, Clark Freeport Zone is now a bustling economic and recreational hub. It features golf courses, casinos, duty-free shops, and recreational parks like Aqua Planet, the Philippines' largest water park.

5.Angeles City Walking Street: Known for its vibrant nightlife, especially along Fields Avenue, Walking Street is lined with bars, clubs, and entertainment venues. It's a lively area where visitors can enjoy music, food, and drinks late into the night.

6.Nayong Pilipino Clark: This cultural theme park showcases replicas of famous Philippine landmarks, traditional houses, and cultural performances. It's a great place to learn about Philippine history and culture in a fun and interactive way.

7.Minalungao National Park: While technically located in Nueva Ecija, Minalungao National Park is easily accessible from Pampanga. It features stunning limestone formations, a river perfect for swimming and bamboo rafting, and hiking trails amidst lush greenery.

8.Cuisine Tour: Pampanga is renowned as the culinary capital of the Philippines, offering a wide array of delectable dishes such as sisig, bringhe (a local version of paella), tamales, and more. Foodies will enjoy exploring local eateries and indulging in authentic Kapampangan cuisine.

Favorite Food

Sisig, a popular Filipino dish made from chopped pig's face and ears, seasoned with calamansi (Philippine lime), onions, and chili peppers, has its roots in Pampanga. Here's a bit more about sisig's connection to Pampanga:

Origins: Sisig is believed to have originated in Angeles City, Pampanga, particularly in a restaurant called Aling Lucing's Sisig. It was invented by Lucia "Aling Lucing" Cunanan in the 1970s. Originally, sisig was just a byproduct of grilling pork masks, cheeks, and heads, but Aling Lucing transformed it into the sizzling and flavorful dish that it is today.
